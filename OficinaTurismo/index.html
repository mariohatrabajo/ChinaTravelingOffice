中國及其傳統

China and its tradition

A country so big but also so diverse, with 9.6km² it houses diverse ecosistems, from the mongol hills to the subtropical forest on the south of the country.

One of the oldest cultures in the world, with an outstanding arquitecture, with the beauty and the functionality of the houses built in the Zhou Dynasty, of beautiful wood.

Or the magestic Great Wall of China, built to keep out the mongol invaders.

Or the lots of cientific discoveries with the invention of the compass, the first seismograph, the gunpowder, etc.

In China there are 8 different languages, the most used is Mandarin wich started being used on the VI century.

Religion is one of the main elements of this culture. The main religions in China are Budism, Confuncianism and the traditional chinese religion, influenced by budism, confuncianism and taoist philosophy.

Taoism is the philosophic system of the chinese culture. Even tho it has its religious branch, it’s mainly a philosophy tought as a “path” (translation of Tao). It is based on the writings of philosophers Lao Tsé and Zhuanti.

美食

Gastronomy

Chinese cuisine is one of the oldest and most diverse cuisines in the world. Two thousand years before the beginning of our era, people there ate noodles. Even today each province has its own cooking style, based on regional ingredients and preferences.

These range from wildly picked greens in the mountains and fresh, salty seafood on the northeast coast to the light, sweet accents of Cantonese cuisine. In Central China, where chilli peppers are rampant, people love hot spicy food, while in Beijing, lamb is a favourite.

Chinese food use staples such as rice, soy sauce, noodles, tea, chili oil, and tofu, and utensils such as chopsticks and the wok.
